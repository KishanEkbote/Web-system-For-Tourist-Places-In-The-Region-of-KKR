Visit The Places At Bellari

Hampi UNESCO

Hampi: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Hampi is an ancient city known for its magnificent ruins, temples, and monuments dating back to the Vijayanagara Empire. Hampi, located in the Bellary district of Karnataka, India, is an ancient city steeped in history, culture, and architectural splendor.

Tungabhadra Dam

The Tungabhadra Dam, situated near Hampi in Karnataka, India, is a significant hydraulic engineering marvel that spans the Tungabhadra River. Constructed to harness the river’s waters for irrigation, hydroelectric power generation, and flood control, the dam serves as a lifeline for the region’s agriculture and economy. Beyond its utilitarian purposes, the Tungabhadra Dam has also emerged as a popular tourist destination, offering visitors a host of recreational opportunities and scenic vistas. The expansive reservoir created by the dam provides a serene backdrop for activities such as boating, fishing, and leisurely picnics along its shores.

Bellari Fort

Bellary Fort, situated in the heart of Bellary, Karnataka, India, is a majestic ancient fortification steeped in history and cultural significance. Constructed centuries ago, the fort stands as a testament to the region’s rich heritage and strategic importance. Perched atop a hill, Bellary Fort offers visitors panoramic views of the cityscape below and the picturesque landscapes that stretch beyond its walls. From its elevated vantage points, one can admire the sprawling city of Bellary and the surrounding natural beauty, providing a captivating backdrop for exploration and contemplation.

Lotus Mahal

Lotus Mahal, nestled within the Zenana Enclosure of Hampi in Karnataka, India, stands as a remarkable architectural gem that exudes elegance and sophistication. This unique structure, dating back to the Vijayanagara Empire, is celebrated for its distinctive lotus-shaped dome and exquisite design. The Lotus Mahal’s architectural style is a beautiful fusion of Hindu and Islamic influences, reflecting the cosmopolitan ethos of the Vijayanagara rulers. Its delicate arches, ornate balconies, and intricate carvings showcase the craftsmanship and artistic finesse of the artisans of yesteryears.
